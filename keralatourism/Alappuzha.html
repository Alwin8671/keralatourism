Tourist Attractions


Alappuzha Beach

Beach lovers must take advantage of Alappuzha’s mesmerizing beauty. Alappuzha Beach is the most famous tourist destination because it is less crowded and picturesque. Dense palm trees, the tranquillity of the surrounding park, freshwater rivers, and the picturesque Arabian Sea are the ideal places to find solitude.

Vembanad Lake

Vembanad Lake is a breathtaking tourist stopover you shouldn’t miss when doing an Alleppey, Kerala, Backwater Tour. One can see the finest of Kerala tourism by taking a houseboat cruise surrounding mangrove forests and coconut palms. On a houseboat, you may go through congested canals, lagoon islands, and teeny hamlets while taking in the simple and tranquil rural way of life.

Backwaters

The Kerala backwaters are a network of brackish lagoons and canals lying parallel to the Arabian Sea of the Malabar coast of Kerala state in south-western India. It also includes interconnected lakes, rivers, and inlets, a labyrinthine system formed by more than 900 km (560 mi) of waterways, and sometimes compared to bayous.[3] The network includes five large lakes linked by canals, both man made and natural, fed by 38 rivers, and extending virtually half the length of Kerala state.
